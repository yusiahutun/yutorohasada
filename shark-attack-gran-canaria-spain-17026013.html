Shark attack: Predator savages lad at Brit holiday beach resort

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  258 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

The 13-year-old lad was reportedly swimming in shallow water off the island of Gran Canaria.

He was rushed to nearby hospital after the attack with serious injuries.

Pictures show the lad’s mutilated foot with tooth-like marks.

Local media said the company that provides lifeguards for the area has refused to confirm whether it was a wound caused by a shark.

It is thought the animal that bit him could be a type of blue shark measuring between 60 and 70cm.

Local officials confirmed they were told of a shark sighting a few days ago.

The government said it will now investigate to ensure to safety of bathers.

Millions of tourists visit the Canary Islands every year.

There are numerous species of shark off the coasts of the Spanish islands, though they are rarely seen by people.

They include hammerheads, makos and bull sharks along with rays and angel sharks.

Officials say there have been five shark attacks in local waters in the past 100 years.

In 2015, Christina Ojeda was left with horrific wounds after a 10ft shark tried to eat her arm on Christmas Day.

The 38-year-old was on a winter holiday in Gran Canaria when she was attacked at Arinaga Beach on the island’s east coast.
