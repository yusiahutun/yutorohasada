Paris recently let the public in on some of her childhood recollections of her godmother in a statement to People magazine.  

She dished that Michael and Elizabeth 'would sit in the living room and have conversations alone. She was very pretty and wore sparkling necklaces and rings.' 

Elizabeth was so fond of jewels she became known as the Queen Of Diamonds and even released a fragrance called White Diamonds. 

Richard Burton, the husband she married twice, once presented her with a whopping 68 carat rock that became known as the Taylor-Burton diamond.

He also gave her the world-famous 33.19 carat Krupp Diamond, which she used to refer to as 'my baby' and which has been rechristened the Elizabeth Taylor Diamond.

Paris dished: 'Most of the time when I would walk into the living room to say: "Goodnight," I was aware of the fact that most of the people who came to see my father looked like business people and they were almost always men.'
