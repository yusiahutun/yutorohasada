It seems things are never quiet on the streets of Philadelphia.

For Anthony Kiedis got involved in a scuffle with a Rolling Stones security guard as he tried to make his way inside his posh hotel in the city on Friday.

The Red Hot Chili Peppers singer was walking into the upmarket Four Seasons with a lady friend when a minder stood in his way.

Street fighting man: Red Hot Chili Peppers singer Anthony Kiedis scuffling with security guards in Philadelphia on Friday

Apparently the guard thought the casually dressed 50-year-old was just a fan of the band, who were leaving the building, and would not budge.

The infuriated Freaky Styley favourite then pushed the guard, who then lunged at him in retaliation.

This then sparked a scuffle between the pair and some other bouncers.

After a short melee, in which a man who waded in in an attempt to aid the musician ended up having his arm put behind his back and pushed into a corner, police came and got things under control.

No harm done: Anthony, who sported a wonderful moustache when he arrived back at the hotel at 3am on Saturday, was magnanimous after the misunderstanding

Slave to the grind: The altercation will have provided a diversion from the monotony of yet another world tour

Not again: For a second it seemed the flip-flop wearing star was going to get involved in another argument

And it seemed to have a happy ending, as Kiedis and the suit-wearing security guard ended up shaking hands at the end of the incident and the frontman made his way into the hotel.

Both the Four Seasons and the Rolling Stones denied the minder worked for either of them, though the latter did speculate he was a local guard hired to supplement their regular team.

And it seems the Under The Bridge singer holds no grudges, as in a statement he said: 'I love the Rolling Stones.'

While he may like the pensioner rockers, he has not been afraid to criticise them in the past, saying: 'They’ve made great music, but they don’t seem like something I'd like us to be.

'They don’t seem to have a real band unity. i don’t know that they enjoy the process as much.'

Old pro: It was as if the pushing session had never happened when he performed with the Chilli Peppers at the Firefly Music Festival that night

Fancy: Both the Red Hot Chili Peppers and the Rolling Stones were staying at the posh Four Seasons Hotel
