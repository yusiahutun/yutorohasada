Toxel’s Pokedex number is 848.

Toxel holds the 848th spot in the National Pokedex, making it a relatively recent addition to the Pokemon universe. Its evolutionary line starts with this charming and energetic Pokemon.

Toxel has a dual typing of Electric/Poison.

This combination of types provides Toxel with a unique set of strengths and weaknesses in battle. Electric attacks are powerful against Flying and Water types, while Poison attacks deal extra damage to Grass and Fairy types.

Toxel is known for its signature ability, Rattled.

Rattled is an ability that triggers when Toxel is hit by a Bug, Dark, or Ghost type move. This ability increases Toxel’s Speed stat, allowing it to outmaneuver its opponents.

Toxel evolves into Toxtricity.

When Toxel reaches a certain level of friendship with its trainer, it will evolve into its final form, Toxtricity. This evolution brings about a change in appearance, abilities, and overall power.

Toxel has two different forms.

There are two forms of Toxel, characterized by their different colors and abilities. The Amped Form is yellow and electric-based, while the Low Key Form is purple and more focused on sound-based attacks.

Toxel’s Amped Form has the Punk Rock ability.

Punk Rock boosts the power of sound-based moves used by Toxel’s Amped Form. This ability makes Toxtricity a formidable opponent in battles against Pokemon weak to sound-based attacks.

Toxel’s Low Key Form has the Technician ability.

Technician increases the power of Toxel’s weaker moves. This ability allows Toxtricity’s Low Key Form to deal surprising damage with its lower-powered attacks.

Toxtricity has two Gigantamax forms.

When Toxtricity Gigantamaxes, it can take on either its Amped Form or its Low Key Form, depending on its original evolved form. Each has its own unique Gigantamax move that deals devastating damage.

Toxtricity is inspired by punk rock music.

Toxtricity’s rebellious and edgy appearance draws inspiration from the punk rock scene. Its design features elements such as a mohawk-like crest, electric guitar-shaped patterns, and an overall punk aesthetic.

Toxtricity’s Gigantamax form resembles a concert stage.

When Toxtricity Gigantamaxes, it transforms into a towering creature with an impressive concert stage background. It showcases its electric personality while unleashing its massive power.

Toxel’s species name is the “Baby Pokemon.”

Known as the Baby Pokemon, Toxel represents the early stage of its evolutionary line. It possesses a playful and curious nature, making it an adorable companion for trainers.

Toxel learns unique moves.

Toxel can learn a variety of moves specific to its species, such as Nuzzle, Acid Spray, Eerie Impulse, and Boomburst. These moves allow it to paralyze, lower opponent’s Special Defense, disrupt their stats, or deal heavy sound-based damage.

Toxtricity’s Dex entry showcases its role in the Galar region’s music scene.

According to its Pokedex entry, Toxtricity is known for its participation in the music scene of the Galar region, captivating crowds with its striking appearance and electrifying performances.

Toxel’s evolution is influenced by its nature.

Toxel’s evolution into either the Amped Form or the Low Key Form depends on its nature. Attack-increasing natures result in the Amped Form, while Special Attack-increasing natures lead to the Low Key Form.

Toxel’s evolutionary line is exclusive to the Pokemon Sword and Shield games.

If you’re playing Pokemon Sword or Shield, you have the special opportunity to train and evolve Toxel and its evolutions as part of your team.

Toxtricity’s signature move is Overdrive.

Toxtricity’s Overdrive move deals Electric-type damage and has a chance to paralyze the target. It is a powerful move that can turn the tide of battle in Toxtricity’s favor.

Toxel’s electric sacs produce unique melodies.

Toxel’s electricity-generating sacs produce melodies that have different effects on other Pokemon. Some melodies can be calming, while others can cause aggression or even raise the spirits of its allies.

Toxel is well-suited for both offensive and support roles in battle.

Thanks to its varied movepool, diverse abilities, and unique typing, Toxtricity can function as a powerful attacker or a supportive Pokemon, depending on the strategy of the trainer.
