Kajiado: 2 Children Cheat Death after Speeding Prado Rams into Their House in Kitengela

 Posted on September 23, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  438 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • Two children narrowly escaped a life-threatening situation when a high-end Toyota Prado TX crashed into several houses in Kitengela
  • Witnesses reported that the driver of the Prado was speeding before losing control of the vehicle
  • Police officers responded to the scene to manage the situation and prevent curious onlookers from interfering
  • Despite the severity of the accident, a police officer reprimanded the crowd for staring and urged them to give space for the authorities to handle the situation

Didacus Malowa, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Kajiado - Two children are counting themselves lucky after having a near-death experience at home.

The two children were reportedly in their house when a high-end Toyota Prado TX rammed into several houses in Kitengela.

According to witnesses, the driver of the Prado was speeding before losing control and driving into houses.

Police officers responded to the situation as they had a difficult time keeping curious onlookers away from the scene.

"What are you all looking at so much? Isn't it just a car that has fallen? Move aside and give us space. Now you're staring, isn't it normal for a car to have fallen?" a police officer told those in the crowd.

What Kipchumba Murkomen said about accidents

The number of road accident fatalities in the country has ignited outrage from a section of political leaders and Kenyans.

During the launch of the National Road Safety Action Plan 2024 - 2028 on April 17, Transport Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen said that whenever there is an accident, Kenyans are always looking for a Cabinet Secretary to blame.

He added that their outrage usually lasts up to a week, and then once a new thing emerges, they focus on it.

He added that the greatest cause of accidents in the country is recklessness and that motorists should begin taking responsibility.

Ruto orders Murkomen to implement fines on roads

As earlier reported, President William Ruto issued a directive to curb the surge of road accidents in the country.

He asked Murkomen to introduce instant road fines as soon as possible.

The head of state added that the government is losing a lot of money treating victims of road accidents and that he's certain that with the support of every arm of the government, accidents in the country will reduce.
