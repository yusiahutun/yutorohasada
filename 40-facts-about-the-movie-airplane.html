Airplane! was released in 1980

Airplane! is a classic comedy film that was released in the year It quickly became a cult favorite and remains popular to this day.

The movie was directed by Jim Abrahams and the Zucker brothers

The talented trio of Jim Abrahams, Jerry Zucker, and David Zucker joined forces to direct Airplane! Their comedic genius shone through in every scene of the film.

The film is a spoof of disaster movies

Airplane! parodies the disaster movie genre, taking jabs at popular films like Airport 1975 and Zero Hour! It cleverly exaggerates the cliches and tropes of these films for comedic effect.

The iconic “Don’t Call Me Shirley” line was ad-libbed

One of the most memorable lines in the movie, “Don’t call me Shirley,” was ad-libbed by Leslie Nielsen, who played the role of Dr. Rumack. It went on to become a well-known catchphrase.

The film features a star-studded cast

Airplane! boasts a fantastic cast, including Robert Hays, Julie Hagerty, Leslie Nielsen, Robert Stack, and Lloyd Bridges. Each actor brought their unique comedic talent to the film.

The movie was a box office success

Airplane! was a commercial success, earning over $83 million at the box office. Its popularity has only grown over the years, cementing its status as a comedy classic.

The Zucker brothers got the inspiration for the film from a disaster movie called Zero Hour!

David and Jerry Zucker were inspired to create Airplane! after watching the disaster movie Zero Hour! They noticed the unintentional humor in the serious dialogue and decided to turn it into a comedy.

The film has countless memorable quotes

Airplane! is filled with memorable quotes that have become ingrained in popular culture. From “Surely, you can’t be serious” to “I am serious. And don’t call me Shirley,” these lines are often quoted by fans.

The script underwent several rewrites

The script for Airplane! went through multiple revisions before it was finalized. The filmmakers worked hard to ensure that every joke landed and the timing was perfect.

The film’s production budget was only $3.5 million

Despite its huge success, Airplane! had a relatively modest budget of $3.5 million. The filmmakers made the most of their resources and created a hilarious and memorable comedy.

The movie is known for its rapid-fire jokes

Airplane! is famous for its rapid-fire jokes and gags. The humor comes at a fast pace, with visual gags, puns, and one-liners keeping the audience laughing throughout the film.

It was nominated for an Academy Award

Airplane! was nominated for Best Writing at the 1981 Academy Awards. While it didn’t win the award, the nomination itself speaks to the film’s clever and witty script.

The film was a critical success

Airplane! received positive reviews from critics upon its release. Its unique brand of humor and clever satire garnered praise and solidified its place as a comedy classic.

Airplane! was a breakthrough film for Leslie Nielsen

Leslie Nielsen’s role in Airplane! marked a turning point in his career. He had previously been known for his serious roles, but his comedic portrayal of Dr. Rumack showcased his talent for comedy.

The movie was shot on a tight schedule

Due to budget constraints, Airplane! was shot in just over a month. The tight schedule meant that the cast and crew had to work efficiently to bring the film to life.

The original title of the film was “Flying High!”

Before settling on Airplane!, the film was originally titled “Flying High!” The title was changed to avoid confusion with another film released around the same time.

The cockpit scenes were filmed in a real airplane

To add authenticity to the film, the cockpit scenes in Airplane! were shot in an actual airplane. This attention to detail helped to enhance the realism of the comedic situations.

The film’s humor has stood the test of time

Even after four decades, Airplane! retains its ability to make audiences laugh. The timeless humor and clever writing ensure that the film remains as funny today as it was upon its release.

Airplane! paved the way for a new wave of comedy films and television shows that parodied various genres. Its success influenced the comedic style of many filmmakers and writers.

The actors had to keep a straight face during hilarious scenes

One of the challenges for the actors in Airplane! was to keep a straight face while delivering hilarious lines and participating in absurd situations. Their comedic timing and professionalism shine through in the finished film.

Airplane! was a breakthrough film for the Zucker brothers

Brothers David and Jerry Zucker, along with Jim Abrahams, gained widespread recognition and success with Airplane! The film marked a turning point in their careers and allowed them to continue making successful comedies.

The film was heavily influenced by the TV show “Police Squad!”

FAQs

Q: Who directed Airplane!?

A: Jim Abrahams and brothers David and Jerry Zucker collectively directed Airplane!, showcasing their comedic genius.

Q: When was Airplane! released?

A: Airplane! was released on July 2, 1980, to critical acclaim and commercial success.

Q: Is Airplane! based on a true story?

A: No, Airplane! is not based on a true story. It is a parody of disaster movies from the 1970s like Airport 1975.
