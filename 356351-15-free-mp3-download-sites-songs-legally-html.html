The internet offers a wide range of free content, including music, photos, video, and text. While one can find many sites with free music, not all of them are legal. Some sites allow the listener to stream but not download, and others allow both. For most people, finding the ideal free music download site that is also legal can be quite daunting. We have created a list of the top MP3 download sites today.

There is a wide range of legal issues surrounding the distribution of music. These issues touch on royalties, rights, and licenses. This makes it important to only download music from legal sites and avoid potential legal problems.

What are the best MP3 download sites?

Here is a look at the best MP3 music download sites.

15. Audiomack

Audiomack is quite appealing to music lovers who love discovering new music. All the music on Audiomack is entirely legal and free to stream. Downloads are artist dependent. The uploading artist can choose to have downloads enabled or disabled.

The Audiomack website is remarkably easy to use. You can search for songs, albums, and artists or browse the top songs and trending sections. The site also has a recently added page that helps users find all the newest music uploaded to the site.

14. Bandcamp

Bandcamp makes it super easy for musicians to share their music in a 'name your price' kind of setting. This means that while listeners can pay for the music, they also have the option to put a zero in the payment box and download the song for free.

Bandcamp has a discover page which is a great way to find the best-selling music on the site. Keep in mind that not all songs can be downloaded for free.

13. BeatStars

BeatStars users can sort the entire site to find newly added songs quickly. One can also filter the displayed results by genre moods, key, instrument, BPM, duration, and energy levels (such as low or very high).

The most notable difference with this site is that for some tracks, you must be subscribed to the artist's profile or social media account before you can access the download link.

12. DatPiff

This is among the best music download sites for rap and hip-hop fans. The site boasts an ever-growing hip-hop community dedicated to creating top-notch music. Interestingly, a lot of the music found on DatPiff comes in the form of mixtapes which can be quite handy for those who do not enjoy saving songs track by track.

11. Free Music Archive

Free Music Archive, also known as FMA, is an excellent free music download site. Unfortunately, the site's user interface is a bit cluttered compared to numerous other alternatives. Still, the site offers a vast collection of songs that one can get for free.

10. Internet Archive

The internet archive is often used as a hilarious reminder of how ridiculous the world wide web looked years ago. Besides that, though, it can also be quite useful in terms of free and legal music downloads. The live music archive section was created in collaboration with etree.org and features numerous concerts and shows by different artists.

All the musicians in this archive have uploaded trade-friendly collections, granting listeners the right to access and use the music for non-commercial purposes.

9. Jamendo

Jamendo is a platform that allows unsigned independent musicians to distribute songs to their fans with ease. The site currently has more than 250,000 royalty-free tracks curated by over 40,000 artists. The music is built around groups known as communities.

8. Last.fm

Last.fm has a huge collection of free song downloads that fall under various genres. Listeners can browse these free downloads by coming soon, categories, new releases, or by browsing through the entire list. Making a preferred selection will automatically save your chosen song. 7. Musopen

Musopen is among the best music download sites you can try if your song taste leans more towards classical music. The site has numerous recordings from some of the most famous classical singers and composers of all time. In addition, listeners will find songs from the likes of Bach, Beethoven, Tchaikovsky, Holst, and others.

6. NoiseTrade

NoiseTrade was designed like a social network for book enthusiasts and music lovers. The site lets artists upload their songs for free. Listeners can then download the songs without having to pay anything. If one likes a certain song, they can choose to tip the artist a few bucks as a token of appreciation. NoiseTrade helps the artists by connecting them with a new fanbase.

5. PureVolume

The PureVolume website was designed around a community of music lovers. Artists can sign up and upload songs to the site. Their fans and other listeners can then help the artists by saving the songs to your device or listening to and upvoting them.

Each artist has their own profile with their basic details, music, and photos. Each artist gets to decide whether to allow or disallow downloads. Most of the artists on the site have the option enabled, so you can enjoy great music for free.

4. ReverbNation

ReverbNation is an excellent site for those looking for songs from artists they don't yet know about. Numerous world-famous bands like Imagine Dragons and The Civil Wars started out on ReverbNation before making it big.

Keep in mind that not every song on the site is downloadable. The ones that can be saved have a small downlink button on the side. Still, all songs can be streamed via a web browser.

3. SoundClick

SoundClick is one of the top free MP3 music download sites today. The artists who upload songs to this site have decided they would like to let people get their music for free. This largely includes unsigned independent artists and a few signed ones.

You can browse through the song charts and genres until you find the music you want to save to your device.

2. SoundCloud

SoundCloud is free and offers one of the largest free online song collections. The site began as an online music streaming community where users could upload songs. The site also allows artists to share their own songs as well. Listeners can stream and save as many songs as they want.

You only need to click on the save button next to a song to download it. It is worth noting that the tracks without the icon next to them cannot be saved for offline listening.

1. YouTube Audio Library

The YouTube Audio Library was designed for people looking for royalty-free production songs to use in videos and other types of productions. Anyone with a YouTube account can access the library via YouTube Studio and download as many songs as they wish.

To find the frees songs, filter the results using the 'creative commons' filter. Alternatively, head into the creator studio and then select the audio library to discover a lot of free tracks. These songs are free to use, and there are no restrictions on downloading them.

Where can I get royalty-free music?

The YouTube Audio Library is inarguably one of the best sites to get royalty-free songs. There are numerous other great alternatives, but most of them require one to pay a subscription fee.

Is there a legal way to download music?

Yes, downloading songs from sites that have been allowed to host downloadable music is legal. These include those listed in this guide and others that require periodic subscriptions.

Is SoundCloud legal?

Yes, the site is licensed by the US National Music Publishers Association (NMPA).

Is illegal downloading a crime?

Yes, in most countries, illegal downloads constitute a crime chargeable in a court of law.

Is the music on SoundCloud copyright free?

Soundcloud allows users to upload songs with a wide range of terms, including those under a Creative Commons license, some of which are royalty-free. To find royalty-free songs, listeners need to filter the available music by this license type.
