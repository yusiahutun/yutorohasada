UFC star Ailin Perez earned $24,000 from her victory against Lucie Pudilova but admits she earned MO

 Posted on October 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  521 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Ailin Perez has revealed she made more money this week through her OnlyFans account compared to the prize purse she claimed, when she beat Lucie Pudilova on Saturday night at UFC Vegas 82.  

It was the third fight of her UFC career, with the 29-year-old joining the organisation back in 2022 before making her debut at UFC Fight Night 209 in September, where she suffered the first loss of her UFC career - a submission defeat against Stephanie Egger. 

Despite that, Perez claimed a dominant victory over Pudilova on Saturday, winning via a unanimous decision, before celebrating by twerking to fans around the Octagon. 


She also subsequently made her OnlyFans account free to fans online again in celebration of the win, claiming it 'was not pornography, it's art'.

Despite that, Argentine fighter has now admitted that she made £4,777 ($6,000) more from her OnlyFans account in the last week compared to the prize fund she earned from the victory.

'Since [this past] Thursday to now, it's been over £23,893 ($30,000),' she said to Ariel Helwani on The MMA Hour, speaking on the funds she's received from OnlyFans.

In contrast, she claimed she received a pay check of £19,114 ($24,000) following the bout, with the MMA star adding that the money she earnt from the online platform enabled her to buy a car. 

'I take advantage of OnlyFans, because that's where I'm [making] my money. With the money I got from OnlyFans, I was able to buy a car.'

'I'm in the UFC because I love to fight. This is the challenge that I've put forth for myself in my life, and I'm fighting in the UFC because I love it. But I'm [making] the money I have on OnlyFans,' she added.

Perez, who hails from Zarate in Buenos Aires, currently has 239,000 subscribers on Instagram and 1,520 on YouTube. 

The bantamweight fighter made her amatuer debut back in 2015 after signing with Invicta Fighting Championships. 

And in celebration of the victory, Perez was seen twerking inside the Octagon while she also made her OnlyFans account free for users online

She currently ranks 11th in the female bantamweight rankings, holding a fight record of 9-2-0. 

Perez is now hoping to extend her stay with the UFC admitting her love of fighting before stating that she will continue to step into the Octagon regardless of the pay. 

'Hopefully I get a new extension, which is what I'm looking for, and hopefully I can multiply that number so it's even better pay than what I've gotten.

'But at this point, if it's more, if it's less, my life is fighting, and I love fighting, and I'm going to fight regardless.'
