Bling it on! Gwyneth Paltrow models four pieces of dainty gold jewelry that total over $7K for GOOP

 Posted on March 12, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  813 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Gwyneth Paltrow was blinged out for a new holiday gift-giving GOOP post shared to Instagram on Wednesday.

The star had on a total of six pieces of jewelry as she sat near a lit Christmas tree in a festive outfit.

But only four of them were recommended as possible Christmas presents.

They were the Shube Signet Pinkie ring that goes for $825, the Charlie Toggle necklace for $4,495, the Tait Pave Ear Cuff for $995 and the Sammy Fan earrings for $945. All are from GOOP's G Label.

The total was $7,260. 

'Shop tiny, shiny adornments (AKA the best gifts),' it read on the video as it said in the caption, 'The kind of subtle-yet-special jewelry they'll never want to take off.'

First the Oscar-winning actress introduced the Shube Signet Pinkie ring that goes for $825.

'It’s no secret that we love a signet ring (hi, Sydney), so introducing a second one to the G. Label Jewelry family just made sense,' the copy online read.

'Crafted from 14-karat yellow gold, Shube riffs on the traditional design with a flat band and an oval face flanked by two prong-set white diamonds. 

'If you wanted to get it engraved, you could take it to a jeweler—though we think it’s achingly chic just the way it is. Whatever you decide, it’s a timeless piece you’ll have (and wear) forever.'

She had on two other gold rings but did not discuss those.

Then went on to the Charlie Toggle necklace that has pave diamonds. She said the diamonds make it 'extra, extra special.' It costs $4,495.

'Wear it like a lariat or a classic chain - whatever your mood calls for,' it says on her GOOP website.

'A comfortable wear-all-day weight, this 14-karat yellow-gold chain comprises a combination of round and oval links that feels both modern and timeless,' the shopping page on GOOP reads.

'The shiny toggle is finished with a double band of white pavé diamonds—an artful detail that adds dimension and shine. Wear it the classic way (with the toggle in the back) or twist it around to the front and pull it through the loop to wear it like a lariat.,' it says on her site.

She then pulled her blonde hair back to show off her Tait Pave Ear Cuff that looked large. It sells for $995.

'Shiny and statement-making, this chunky ear cuff has graceful tapered ends and a line of teensy white pavé diamonds down the middle. 

'Rendered in lustrous 14-karat yellow gold, it has a small opening for secure wear—slip it on near the top of your ear and slide it down to the desired placement. It’s especially chic, we think, worn on its own without anything else in the ear,' it reads on her site.

On her other ear were the two Sammy Fan earrings that sell for $945.

'Rendered in 14-karat yellow gold with a simple post back, these sleek, fan-shaped studs boast high-octane shine,' the description read.  

'The clean, modern lines complement casual and dressy looks alike. 

'And while they’re intended to be worn in the lobes, Gwyneth discovered an alternate use for them on the set of our shoot: You can wear a single earring up in the helix, so the curved bottom edge curls around the cartilage,' GOOP states.

'I have not taken this off since we came out with it,' she said.

Paltrow wore a button down and belted cream colored cardigan that was unbuttoned on the bottom and she added red slacks for a holiday glow.

Her flaxen hair was perfectly styled and her makeup was a bronze glow with black eye liner and pink lipstick.

She was seated on an off white chair next to a Christmas tree with lights and no ornaments.
