Michelle Pfeiffer and Al Pacino reunite for 35th anniversary celebration of gangster movie Scarface

 Posted on May 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  633 words  |  Aldo Pusey

It's been 35 years since Michelle Pfeiffer and Al Pacino starred together in the film Scarface.

She was a 25-year-old unknown actress and he was the 43-year-old acclaimed star of The Godfather Parts 1 and II. 

And on Thursday night they reunited for a special celebration for the Brian De Palma  gangster movie at the Tribeca Film Festival in New York.

Legends: It's been 35 years since Michelle Pfeiffer and Al Pacino starred together in Scarface and on Thursday night they reunited for a special celebration at the Tribeca Film Festival

A classic: She was a 25-year-old unknown actress and he was the 43-year-old acclaimed star of The Godfather Parts 1 and II when the acclaimed gangster movie came out

They were joined at the reunion by De Palma and co-star Steven Bauer.

Pacino, now 77, wore a baggy black jacket with baggy black trousers and a black shirt.

He added a white scarf for a dapper touch.

Pfeiffer, 59, also opted for a black ensemble pairing a tailored jacket with tailored pants and a pinstripe shirt.

Ageless: Pfeiffer defied her 59 years, looking as radiant as she did in the iconic movie [pictured L in 1983 and R on Thursday]

Legend: Pacino swapped a rifle for the microphone at the reunion chat [L in the 1983 movie and R on Thursday]

Together again: Pfeiffer, 59, and Pacino, 77, posed for photos together ahead of the screening and panel discussion [L in Scarface, 1983]

Bauer, 69, paired skinny black jeans with a navy shirt, black jacket and a long black patterned scarf.

Scarface, written by Oliver Stone, is set in Miami in 1980 with Pacino as a determined Cuban immigrant Tony Montana who takes over a drug cartel and succumbs to greed. 

Bauer plays his best friend Manny Ribera who escaped with him from Cuba and Pfeiffer plays Elvira, the woman he marries after he murders her husband, played by Robert Loggia.

Infamous couple: Pfeiffer played Elvira, the woman Cuban immigrant Tony Montana, played by Pacino, marries after he murders her husband

Co-star: Steven Bauer, who played Pacino's best friend in the film, joined in the celebration wearing skinny black jeans with a navy shirt, black jacket and a long black patterned scarf

Natural beauty: Pfeiffer opted for a black ensemble pairing a tailored jacket with tailored pants and a pinstripe shirt. Her blonde hair fell in loose ringlets around her shoulders

He called the  shots: Scarface director Brian De Palma, third from left, joined his stars at the film festival

During the panel discussion at the film festival, moderator Jesse Kornbluth was booed after he asked Pfeiffer how much she weighed during filming, the NY Daily News reported.  

Audience members heckled him with shouts of 'Why do you need to know!?' and 'Why!' 

Pfeiffer took the question in her stride and explained she didn't know how much she weighed but she was required to be thin because her character was a cocaine addict.

'I was starving by the end of this movie,' she told the audience.  

'I literally had members of the crew giving me bagels because they were all worried about me and how thin I was getting.'
