Biography

Yoshiki Sasai was born in Japan on March 5, 1962. Known for his work in the biology sub-field of stem cell research, this prominent Japanese scientist was the first to successfully grow an embryonic optic cup from human stem cells. He taught at Kyoto University and directed a department of the RIKEN research institute. He and Nobel Prize winner Osamu Shimomura were both prominent Japanese scientists. After graduating from Kyoto University’s School of Medicine, he accepted a research fellowship at the University of California- Los Angeles’ medical school.
