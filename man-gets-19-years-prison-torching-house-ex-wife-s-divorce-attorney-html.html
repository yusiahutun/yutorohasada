Man gets 19 years in prison for torching the house of his ex-wife's divorce attorney

 Posted on April 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  510 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

An Idaho man has been sentenced to 19 years in prison for setting fire to the home of his ex-wife’s divorce attorney and her police officer husband.

District Judge Dane Watkins Jr. ruled Thursday that Melvin Savage, 44, could be released from prison on parole after serving for four years.

Savage apologized to a teary-eyed courtroom for setting alight in December the home of Idaho Falls lawyer Laurie Gaffney.

Fire starter: Melvin Savage (left), 44, has been sentenced to 19 years with the possibility of parole after four years for an arson attack targeting the home of his ex-wife's divorce attorney, Laurie Gaffney (right) 

Police said that Savage went to the attorney's home at about 1.30am December 15 and lit a gas can on fire near a window well.

Gaffney, who represented Savage’s ex-wife during the couple's divorce proceedings, said she was awake and worried for her husband and two children, ages 10 and 13, that night.

An Idaho Falls Police officer responded to the attorney's 911 call and helped her husband, police officer Eric Andersen, extinguish the flames with a pot of soapy water. The family’s home in the 4000 block of Cambria Drive sustained no significant damage.

Savage fled to Colorado, but was arrested at a motel in Loveland less than a day after the fire. In late March, he pleaded guilty to first-degree arson.

Speaking to The Idaho Statesman back in January, Laurie Gaffney said the arson had been the culmination of a months-long harassment and intimidation campaign carried out by Melvin Savage.

According to the lawyer, Savage had been sending her threatening emails all throughout the yearlong divorce proceedings, which she described as ‘hostile.’

Under attack: Savage went to the attorney's home December 15 and lit a gas can on fire near a window well while Gafney was inside with her husband, police officer Eric Andersen, and his two children 

Andersen was able to extinguish the flames, and the entire family escaped unharmed 

Savage, who according to his divorce attorney and close friend had been struggling with alcohol dependency, had been incessantly calling his estranged wife and demanding to see his children.

In November, after receiving one of Savage's menacing missives, Laurie Gaffney filed a complaint and had Savage arrested and charged with misdemeanor phone harassment

A week after his release from jail, Eric Andersen, Gaffney's husband and father of two, had one of the tires of his car slashed in their driveway.

The perpetrator, believed by the family to have been Savage, returned twice more in two weeks to cut the tires on Andersen's vehicle.

‘It got to the point that I couldn't sleep at night,’ she said. ‘I was scared for my life and the life of my family.’

In court Thursday, Melvin Savage said he is embarrassed by his actions. His mother told the Post Register he feels great remorse.
