Norwegian Royal Family Fun Facts

 Posted on August 5, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  511 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

You probably know a lot about the British royal family, and you might have even heard about Sweden's sexy monarchs, but how much do you know about the Norwegian royal family? Well, listen up, because they're awesome.

1) When King Harald risked it all for romance.

The head of the Norwegian royal family is King Harald V, 80, who ascended the throne in 1991, and his wife, Queen Sonja, 79. Their marriage in 1968 caused controversy because Sonja, the daughter of a clothing merchant, was a commoner. According to the official House of Norway website, Sonja had a degree in dressmaking and tailoring and dated Harald in secret for nine years before they were given permission to marry. Apparently, Harald told his father, King Olav V, that if he couldn't marry Sonja he would never marry at all, leaving their line without an heir.

2) When the Crown Prince followed in his father's footsteps.

King Harald's only son and heir to the throne is Prince Haakon, 43. He's also a looker, but sadly, he's taken. Just like his father, Haakon married a commoner, Mette-Marit Tjessem Høiby, in 2001. Many believed Mette-Marit was unsuitable for the throne because she was a single mother and college dropout, and the father of her son, Marius Borg Høiby, had been convicted for drug possession. But, just like his father, Haakon loved her and married her anyway. Did I mention that he met her in 1996 at the largest rock festival in Norway?

4) When Prince Haakon shaved his beard halfway through his parents' 80th birthday gala as a joke.

The lavish, two-day celebration for the King and Queen's 80th birthdays kicked off on May 9, 2017, with royals from all over the world flocking to the palace for a glittering, tiara-filled banquet. Halfway through the glamorous event, Haakon shaved his beard "as part of the entertainment during the gala dinner.”
