Easter is one of the most cherished holidays among Christians. It is a period of honouring the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. How to celebrate Easter varies from one religion to another.

During the Easter holidays, Christians celebrate the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. This selfless undertaking in a Christian's life dates back several centuries ago. How to celebrate Easter depends on various factors. However, maintaining the essence of the holiday is the key.

When is Easter in 2024?

Easter is observed on the first Sunday after the first full moon. This occurs after the spring equinox on 21st March every year. Therefore, it can fall on any Sunday between 22nd March and 25th April. In 2024, Easter will be observed on Sunday, 31st of M.

How to celebrate Easter

Easter celebrations vary from one country to another and from one religion to the other. However, here are a few traditions, ideas and religious activities on how to celebrate Easter.

Easter traditions

After celebrating Ash Wednesday, the Protestant, Roman Catholicism, and Western Christianity group entered a Lent period. It is marked by repentance and fasting for 40 days excluding Sundays. The last Sunday that happens right before Easter Sunday is referred to as Palm Sunday.

The day is used in commemoration of Jesus' triumphant entry into Jerusalem. During this day, Christians engage in various religious activities such as carrying palm leaves and singing.

Easter celebrations activities

How is Easter celebrated around the world? Christians across the world hold special church services while others light candles, put music, and flowers. Even though the celebrations may vary, they are heightened by spiritual thanksgiving and different family fanfare activities.

During Good Friday and Easter Monday, people attend church prayers services. Different religions celebrate the holidays differently as follows:

Jehovah Witnesses

Do Jehovah Witnesses celebrate Easter? No, they don't. Their belief is based solely on the scriptures. Therefore, they tend not to adhere to any religious creeds of human speculations. They believe that this festival is based on pagan customs and religions.

Seventh-Day Adventists

Do Seventh-day Adventists celebrate Easter? No, they do not officially celebrate the festival. The Seventh-day Adventists believe that the Holy Scriptures are God's revelation to humanity. As a result, the Bible is used as their sole rule and guideline for their faith and practice.

Jewish

Do Jewish Christians celebrate Easter? Yes, they do. They were the first group of people to celebrate the resurrection of Jesus Christ. These celebrations were timed concerning the Passover.

It is a celebration of the liberation of the ancient Israelites from the Egyptian bondage and their exodus. How do the Jewish celebrate Easter? They celebrate the festival through prayers and holding church services.

Protestant

Easter Sunday is used to mark the beginning of the Eastertide, which is also known as the Easter season in some protestant denominations. The season comes to an end on the 50th day after Easter, and this is also referred to as the Pentecost Sunday.

How do the Protestants celebrate Easter? The Protestant's official celebration of the resurrection of Jesus Christ is marked through the Easter Prayer Vigil. This begins during the sunset on Saturday before Easter Sunday.

During this period, some protestants hold scripted prayer services in their churches. Others offer prayer vigils through the night to the worship service on Easter morning.

Orthodox

Easter Sunday holidays mark the beginning of Easter, also known as the season of Pascha for the Orthodox. The season ends after 40 days with the celebrations of a holiday known as the Ascension Feast.

  • Spending quality time with your loved ones.
  • Buying custom-made gifts for Easter.
  • Recreating the Good Friday and Easter Monday events.
  • Crafting home-made Easter cards.
  • Wearing new clothes to symbolize the beginning of a new life with Jesus Christ.
