#TheBrand | Ren Beauty - Make-up artist Nikki DeRoest interview

 Posted on March 27, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  950 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

In our regular feature #TheBrand, Bazaar’s digital beauty director looks into an exciting and efficacious brand taking the beauty industry by storm. This time, it’s Róen Beauty, the clean beauty brainchild of celebrity make-up artist Nikki DeRoest.

The latest LA export catching make-up magpies’ eyes is the seductive Róen Beauty. It launched with an edit of opulent, pigment-packed eye shadows and has since expanded into brow pencils – with plenty more colour cosmetics in the works.

What’s the story?

Nikki DeRoest is the talent behind it, a high-profile make-up artist whose work you will have seen on the faces of Hailey Bieber, Bella Hadid and–her good friend–Rosie Huntington-Whiteley. DeRoest’s signature is glowing skin, groomed brows and, often, a gleam of shimmer.

“When I say Róen Beauty is cruelty-free I laugh that instead of testing on animals, I always test on celebrities! They’re always the first to get to use the products,” DeRoest tells us. And they’re not safe when Huntington-Whiteley is around. “Rosie steals everything from me and half the time she’ll steal stuff I only have one or two of! My staff are like, ‘Why does Rosie have that, and we don’t?’ – I actually have to watch my kit when I’m packing up.”

DeRoest describes Róen Beauty as her “curation of ‘the best of the best’" – or what she thinks is "missing from the market”. It’s her innovative tweaks on the individual categories, as she explains: “My idea is making make-up for the woman that wants to look amazing and glamorous but is on-the-go and wants to get from A to B as quickly as possible.” This is also why the eye shadow shades she created “are ones that you’ll actually wear”, and the reason why she only made three shades in her brow pencils - “because as an artist those are the three shades I always grab for different skin tones and different hair colours, including redheads.” Róen Beauty is about uncomplicated, modern glamour.

DeRoest’s inspiration not only comes from her clientele (“I dream up a lot as I’m working with different people”) and the needs she hears from women, but also her penchant for twinkling jewels – her second passion. “I just can’t help myself, I’ve loved anything shiny since I was young. My mum can attest to all the glitter that she still finds around the house. Jewellery and metals – there’s something that really mesmerises me with the shine,” she says. This clearly plays into Róen’s amplified eye shadows; they’re to be worn as accessories in their own right.

The brand ethos

Róen Beauty isn’t calling itself ‘sustainable luxury’, but it aspires to be. “We are trying our best to do what we can in that regard, but it definitely is tough. It’s hard to get the best of everything,” DeRoest explains. “It’s a constant conversation with our suppliers; where we’re sourcing things, and packaging-wise what we can do.”

What they do offer is “clean, cruelty-free make-up that’s luxurious”. She says that, especially when it comes to colour cosmetics, “it’s really hard to achieve ‘clean’ make-up but keep it glamorous and have performance”. In the past a lot of the products aiming to do this simply haven’t succeeded.

Where should we start?

Called Lid Illumes, the eye shadows deliver pigments with a foil effect. “It’s not glitter, it’s called pearl reflex.” Technically, this comes from mica but DeRoest’s team are very careful about where it’s sauced. “Mica is such a big conversation right now in beauty, but our lab in LA sends us reports every month detailing where it is sourced so we can make sure that it’s sustainable and ethical with fair trade practices.”

At Cult Beauty you’ll find the Warm Eyeshadow Palette (think burnished tones), Cool Eyeshadow Palette (frosted tones), and two versions of the Disco Eye, a grown-up glitzy ‘topper’ which lends a veil of sparkle to any eyeshadow underneath, or simply bare lids.

When it comes to brows, the new VowBrow Pencil (in Light, Medium or Dark) has a fine tip and firm formula to allow you to stroke on realistic looking hairs, which you can blend with the spoolie brush on the other end.

DeRoest shares her secrets with us. “A step that probably everyone doesn’t know is to powder the brows with a translucent powder before doing a brow application. In this day and age we’re all really crazy about amazing skincare, which is great, but sometimes it makes it a challenge for make-up application. So, if you dust a little powder on first, it not only helps to start to thicken the brows but then the pencil can also grip to the skin a lot better.”

She also likes to fill in the outer half of the eyebrow first, “because that's generally where we need more product,” the artist explains. “I do both sides on the outer part, take a look, and see what you actually need on the inner part of you brow. Because typically it’s thinner there, and too much product on the inner part of the brow can weigh the face down.”

It chimes with her – and Róen’s – ethos of only using make-up where it’s actually needed. “So much of the time we go on autopilot, but you should really assess where it’s needed,” she instructs. And that is how you her nail cool-girl glamour: go for glitz, but leave some things bare. It’s the ultimate beauty balancing act.
