Taylor Swift Reveals She Wants a Minimum of Four Kids

 Posted on March 23, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  358 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Taylor Swift has ambitious desires when it comes to family. The singer, who at 22 years old has landed on Forbes' list of highest-paid female celebrities, revealed that she wants a minimum of four kids. She is planning on a brood and is looking forward to the pitter-patter of at least eight little feet.

Swift reportedly joked that all the cash she is raking in as a well-paid celeb will all go towards a good cause: her future kids. "That money will be really good for sending my kids to college some day -- or maybe I'll have a few more kids than I thought. I want a bunch of them running around, minimum four," she says in the new issue of Marie Claire, according to Monsters and Critics.

Swift did admit in her 'Nightline' interview that she is already worrying about what school she wants to send her unborn kids to, so it makes sense that she is probably putting aside her hard-earned cash for their college funds now, as opposed to later. She thinks ahead... A lot!

While Swift does want no less than four tots -- a far cry from the 10 she wished for in an interview last fall -- she isn't in any rush to get there. She isn't going to start reproducing just yet, admitting to the U.K. edition of Marie Claire that, "I want to wait a while, but the idea of pouring everything you are into another person when you become a parent has always been amazing to me."

The 'Begin Again' singer remains a hopeless romantic at heart, despite spilling about her numerous high-profile, failed relationships in her songs, kissing and telling while never revealing who she is singing about.

"[I believe in love] even after it explodes into a million pieces and burns down and you're standing in a pile of the ash of what it once was thinking, 'Why did I have to meet this person, why did this have to happen?'" she says. "But then, when you make eye contact with someone across the room and it clicks and, bam, you're there. In love again."
