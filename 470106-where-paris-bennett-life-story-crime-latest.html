Paris Bennett's story began on 5th February 2007, at around 12:30 am, when Charity Lee received a call while at work informing her that her younger child had been hurt. Charity had left Paris and Ella under the care of a babysitter.

Cunningly, Paris convinced the babysitter to leave, allowing him room to harm his sister. After the babysitter left, Paris attacked Ella, who was in her bedroom. He choked her and sexually assaulted her before stabbing her 17 times.

After all this, he spoke to a friend for six minutes before calling the police to report the incident. They arrived immediately and arrested the teenage boy. He alleged that he was suffering a hallucination and that he saw a demonic version of his younger sister.

However, the following day, he stated that the murder was a calculated move. He admitted that he killed his younger sister to cause pain and misery to his mother.

Paris Bennett's legal proceedings

After being apprehended, Paris faced allegations of committing capital murder against Ella. Due to his age, which fell below the minimum threshold for trying juveniles as adults in Texas (set at 14), he was prosecuted in juvenile court. He admitted guilt to the charge of capital murder.

The court imposed a 40-year prison sentence on Paris, allowing for the potential of parole after serving 20 years. This represents the maximum penalty for a juvenile court conviction of capital murder in Texas.

Upon reaching the age of 19, Paris was transferred to an adult prison. Throughout the legal proceedings, he received a diagnosis of psychopathy, and he disclosed experiencing homicidal thoughts since childhood.

Paris Bennett's interview

In 2019, Paris held an interview with Piers Morgan, where he once again confessed to deliberately murdering his younger sister. He claimed that his initial thoughts were targeted against his mother but later changed his mind.

He confessed that:

For many years, there was just this hot, flaming ball of wrath in the pit of my stomach, and it was directed at my mother. And one of the reasons why I chose to kill my sister and not someone else is because I knew that by doing that, I could hurt my mother in the worst possible way. After all, as a child, I had always known that the most devastating thing to my mother would be the loss of one of her children, and I found a way to take away both her children in one fell swoop.
