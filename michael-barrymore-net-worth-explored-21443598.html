Michael Barrymore net worth explored amid new Channel 4 documentary

 Posted on March 2, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  613 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

TV presenter Michael Barrymore’s career was derailed in 2001 when a man was found dead in his pool.

Before then, Barrymore had been one of the most popular entertainers in the UK, presenting shows such as Strike It Lucky.

Tonight a Channel 4 documentary called Barrymore: The Body in the Pool, which re-examined what happened that night.

Stuart Lubbock had been invited back to Barrymore’s house for party after they met at a nightclub in Essex, and was later found dead in the entertainer’s swimming pool.

Six years later, in 2007, Barrymore was arrested in connection with the death of the 31-year-old. He was released by police without charge.

A High Court judge found there were no reasonable grounds to suspect him.

However, Barrymore lost his contract with ITV in the aftermath of the death and controversy.

The former presenter kept a low profile after the ordeal, and has always maintained his innocence.

What is Michael Barrymore’s net worth:

Michael Barrymore’s net worth is estimated to be $15million (£11million), according to the website NetWorthPost.

However, other sites claim his net worth is “under review”.

At the height of his fame he was reported to earn up to £2million a year from ITV.

At this time he lived in a mansion and had a collection of 17 luxury cars.

Is has been claimed he was worth at least £9million by the time he and his wife Cheryl split in 1997.

The TV star gave her £1.5million as well as their £2million London townhouse.

After he became the centre of controversy, Barrymore moved to New Zealand with his partner Shaun Davis.

Since moving back to the UK Barrymore reportedly lives in modesty in Acton, West London.

According to the Mirror, he is living in a one bedroom home in a gated area.

In 2006 he returned to the public eye briefly to appear on Celebrity Big Brother in the UK, which he earned a whopping £150,000 for. The former presenter finished as runner-up.

Since then he has also featured on shows such as The Friday Night Project and Come Dine With Me.

Barrymore was also paid £300,000 to write his autobiography Awight Now: Setting the Record Straight.

In 2016 Barrymore sued Essex Police for £2.4million. He was seeking “aggravated and exemplary damages” for his “wrongful arrest and detention”.

He claimed this had caused him “loss and damage, namely distress, shock, anxiety and damage to his reputation.”

The Court of Appeal ruled in December 2018 that he was only entitled to “nominal damages”.

Announcing the decision, Sir Brian Leveson, declared Barrymore was “entitled to nominal damages only.”

Last year an Essex Police spokeswoman said the entertainer’s claim had been discontinued by agreement, and the force had not made any payment to him.

A statement from Essex Police said: “Following a ruling made by the Court of Appeal in December 2018, and upon agreement having been reached by the parties, the High Court has ordered [by consent] that Mr Parker discontinues the entirety of his claim and all associated issues against the chief constable of Essex Police and that each party will bear its own costs in the proceedings.

“Essex Police has not made and will not make any payment to Michael Parker. We welcome the conclusion of this litigation.”

Barrymore was due to appear on Dancing on Ice 2020, but withdrew before it even started due to injury.

Barrymore: The Body In The Pool airs tonight at 9pm on Channel 4
