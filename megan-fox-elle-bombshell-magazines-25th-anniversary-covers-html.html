She is the archetypal Hollywood cover girl.

And while her acting career to date is memorable mainly for popcorn franchise Transformers, Megan Fox certainly fits the description accorded her by magazine Elle, The Bombshell.

Newlywed Megan, 24, is one of four stars under 25 gracing the cover of the magazine's in celebration of reaching its milestone 25th anniversary issue.

And inside, the magazine profiles 25 women under 25 on four different covers.

Brunette bombshell: Megan Fox is one of Elle magazine's 25 under 25 stars, and one of four celebrities on the cover of the British magazine's October issue

The magazine selected the top four stars under 25. But Amanda Seyfried is actually already 25 and another, Gabourey Sidibe, is 27.

Just Megan, 24, and former The Hills star  Lauren Conrad, 24 are under 25.

Lauren, who is reportedly in talks to return to television after leaving The Hills said the best part about being in her twenties is that it’s her time to be 'selfish.'

While Mamma Mia! and Dear John star Amanda admitted that she is scared of life after 25.

'The worst is that I’m scared; the scariest thing is that I know it can’t be easy—the years to come,' she said.

Gabourey Sidibe's cover has drawn criticism for its unconventional style in comparision to the other three classic cover girl poses

However, while Megan looks typically flawless, in a classic cover girl pose, Gabourey Sidibe's close-up headshot has sparked accusations of tokenism.

The less than flattering cover photo is something of a departure from the magazine's usual style, and the  Precious actress's head is so big on the cover she even covers the magazine's name up.

Writer Mary Elizabeth Williams argues on Salon.com: 'Elle's inclusion of her feels less like a celebration of non-traditional beauty than a token gesture of diversity.'

While one disappointed commenter on blog Young Black & Fabulous wrote: 'In comparison to the other covers, she looks horrible. Without comparing her cover to the others she still looks horrible.

A-list typical: The Hills Lauren Conrad and Dear John actress Amanda Seyfried

'I'm sorry but I'm not of the opinion that obesity (and bad wigs) should be celebrated. Elle could have picked a young, black, and TRULY fabulous celebrity and done MUCH better if they really wanted to. This... seems like mockery.'

But Gabourey is somewhat used to having to fend off criticism. The young actress whose role in Precious earned rave reviews and an Oscar nomination, admitted to feeling the pressure of age in Hollywood, especially playing a teenager on screen.

She said: 'Part of me thought it would be weird if people knew I was a grown-up playing a teenager in ‘Precious,’' she said.

'So I had a twenty-first-birthday party (instead of a 25th).' But even for Fox, her age is an issue, especially when it comes to questions about her new husband, Brian Austin Green.

'When I talk about my husband, I feel as if people roll their eyes,' she said.

'It’s like when you’re 16 and order a martini, and the waiter says, "Do you think I’m stupid?" They can’t grasp that I’m old enough to be married.'

Other women recognised on the list include Olympic medallist Lindsey Vonn, Mila Kunis, Meghan McCain and singer Janelle Monae.
