Stacey Baca Career

Stacey started her career in 1991 by working as a Staff Writer with The Denver Post. She worked closely with Denver Police Department and covered Adams County beats. After 4 years she worked with News Channel 3 Norfolk as News Anchor and Reporter. She talked about covering crime, city hall, and severe weather news. In December 1998 she began working for NBC 7/39, San Diego. She worked for three years with them and anchored NBC 7 San Diego’s weekend morning show. Even for the fun work, she covered the 2000 summer Olympics in Australia. She has also covered content on drugs, smuggling, international border issue, military issue, and deployments. In 2002 she began working for ABC7 Chicago as News Anchor and Reporter. She had reporting experience in the United States and Mexico.

Along with that, she was part of many galas and fundraisers for community organizations. She has also covered news about covid pandemic from every angle. Recently in 2022 she announced her decision to leave ABC 7 and thanked the viewers on Eyewitness News Sunday morning when her show was being aired. Stacey posted a video on her Instagram handle to disclose the hardest decision ever in her career to leave ABC7 Chicago. She said that she definitely loves her job but her family is more important. She added that she would dedicate some time to her husband and family and would like to travel across the world. Stacey would be making her last appearance on 26th June 2022 so we wish her the best for rest of her life.
