  • Potential candidates have until April 17 to submit their applications for the posts advertised on Friday, April 12

Nairobi: Arizona International College has dangled job vacancies for qualified Kenyans.

The premier tertiary institute, which operates as a Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) college, is located in Mlolongo.

Qualification for new jobs at Arizona International College

Needed are customer care and marketing executives.

The institution stipulated that the potential candidates have until April 17 to submit their applications.

A bachelor's degree or diploma in journalism, communication, public relations, or a related field was among the qualifications.

Applications would be made to opportunities@arizonainternationalcollege.africa.

Fraudulent college busted in Nairobi

Separately, the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) launched a probe into the Branka International College accused of robbing its students of their monies.

The country's top investigations body said it was receiving numerous complaints from the students of the institution situated along Nairobi's Mfangano Road, Hakati House.

The officers instituted their investigations; obtaining money by false pretence is among the accusations the college faced.

The detectives, in the course of their work, established that Branka International has not been duly registered since 2021 when it started admitting students.

According to the detectives, the college's attempts to be included in the TVET Management Information System (TVET MIS) failed.

Even with the failed registration, the institution continued to operate.

While registering students, Branka International guaranteed them job placements in the USA, Europe and Australia.

The institution was said to charge fees of KSh 350,000 per student. Courses were taught within a week, after which students waited for their credentials and the foregoing opportunities in overseas countries.

According to the DCI, an estimated KSh 70 million from the victim students could not be accounted for.
