The 18 Best Shampoos for Wavy Hair of 2024

 Posted on September 17, 2024  |  24 minutes  |  4967 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Of all the different hair types out there, wavy hair is one of the most difficult to define. It’s not quite curly, yet it’s not quite straight, which places it in a nebulous state, landing somewhere in between the two. Since it’s not one or the other, that can make it difficult to find the right products and tools like hair masks, hair dryers, and, most infuriatingly, shampoos.

And though the versatility of wavy hair gives you free range to master practically any style, trying to do so is an exercise in futility if you’re not using the right formula that caters to your specific texture, length, and hair density. Sudsing up with something a little too heavy can cause finer wavy hair to look oily fast, but using something too lightweight may not give you that satisfying squeaky clean feeling. Fortunately, we found experts who specialize in understanding what it takes to tame waves and smooth tresses while enhancing your natural curl pattern.

Suffice it to say, there’s a lot happening here, and even though all of these ingredients are lightweight, we’d still lather with about a  dime-sized amount of product to ensure you’re not loading down your hair with too many oils.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Ingredients: “Wavy hair tends to dry out faster,” says Taylor, so finding a shampoo that is jam-packed with hydrating oils is key. However, both Taylor and Dueñas agree that creamy or rich shampoos should be avoided or used in small amounts to prevent hair from getting weighed down. Instead, Dueñas recommends looking for products that contain coconut oil, argan oil, and jojoba oil, while Taylor says formulas that contain glycerin and plant-based butters, such as shea and cocoa, along with essential oils, like lavender and almond, also work together beautifully to maintain frizz. 
  • Hair Texture: Hair texture plays an important role in what formula will work best for you, but it’s equally important to assess what your strands need, says Taylor. For example, if you have fine and thin hair that’s also dry, Taylor recommends looking for a caffeine-infused shampoo with moisturizing ingredients, as it helps with volume in the hair, she says. Another simple tip for anyone with fine hair to follow, according to Dueñas, is to look for formulas with oils that are listed further down the ingredient list, which means they’re being used at a lower concentration, and therefore less likely to leave hair feeling heavy. However, if you have thick or coarse hair, don’t be shy about using shampoos that list oils as the first or second ingredient. Humectants like glycerine, or hydrating butters also work wonders on coarse textures. 
  • Sulfate-Free Formulas: Wavy hair thrives with hydration, so one surefire way to keep it healthy and happy is to opt for a sulfate-free formula—especially in shampoos for damaged hair. “These shampoos prevent the removal of moisture while still cleaning the hair,” says hairstylist and Digital Artistic Educator for Schwarzkopf Brendnetta Ashley. Instead, you keep an eye out for plant-derived surfactants, like those found in palm fruits, or formulas that contain ingredients with natural antibacterial properties, such as aloe and witch hazel, that gently dissolve oils and dirt.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you wash wavy hair? 

Our experts agree that washing wavy hair more than two times a week could strip it of the natural oils it needs to stay healthy, and washing too often can “cause dry brittle hair that can lead to breakage,” says Ashley. Instead, focus on how much time you spend in the shower during wash days. Ashley says you should double rinse with your shampoo, starting with a quick wash before going in a second time and scrubbing longer.

Taylor always recommends using a super moisturizing conditioner after every wash to help detangle the hair. However, if your hair is particularly dry and frizzy, try “mixing a small amount of conditioner in with your shampoo to hydrate and cleanse at the same time,” says Dueñas. And while your wavy hair certainly needs hydration, relying on too many oils too often can lead to product buildup, which is where a really great clarifying shampoo comes in handy. “I would recommend using a detox shampoo first and following it up with a moisturizing shampoo,” says Ashley.

What makes wavy hair less frizzy?

Taylor recommends washing wavy hair in cold water to prevent frizz. “Lower temperatures protect your hair and prevent overdrying and flyaways,” she says, adding that you can finish off your routine by brushing through your hair with a wide-toothed comb. This avoids any tugging or snagging as you gently detangle hair. Using microfiber hair towels made from finer fibers will also cause less friction and pulling on your hair, says Taylor, which can result in frizz.

After washing your hair, try to avoid touching it before it’s fully dry, says Ashley. “If the hair is not dry and you're constantly touching or tousling your hair, you will create frizz.” 

How do you dry wavy hair?

The easiest way to style wavy hair is by letting it air dry — albeit the technique requires a little more patience. “The key to letting it air dry is using a leave-in conditioner to help tame flyaways and define your hair’s natural texture,” says Taylor. To enhance your waves, Dueñas says that you can scrunch your wet hair with a t-shirt or microfiber towel or braid your strands into large sections while you wait for them to dry. 

On days that require heat, a diffuser is your best bet at seeing those defined, bouncy waves come to life. Ashley recommends starting with your dryer on medium heat and airflow as you work it around your head, being careful not to “fully insert it into the hair as the outer cast of the hair needs to dry first.” For more volume, “flip your head upside down and place the diffuser into your hair,” she adds.

Don’t forget to finish with a cool shot, says Taylor. “This locks the look together and helps the style last longer.”
