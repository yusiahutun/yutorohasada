The new way to cut your hair

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  341 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Paul Zak

Photographer: Paul Zak for Harper's Bazaar UK

In the best New York salons thermal scissors aren't just in demand, they're a necessity. To appreciate and understand the difference between this new heated approach and a pair of traditional scissors, Beatrice Aiden paid a visit to RYS salon in Walton Street, London, where the NYC trend is making waves.

What are the benefits of thermal scissors?

The heated blades seal and round off the end of the hair to trap in moisture. A haircut will last and extra month, split-end free. The alternative? Traditional scissors leave blunt ends exposed to pollution and sunlight, which leads to visible damage and split ends – not helped by high-heat hair-drying techniques and heat-protection-free adventures with GHD straighteners.

What to expect…

When it comes to the cut, scissors are scissors, right? Not, I discovered, when they belong Yilmaz Sahin, the director stylist of RYS salon, and are plugged in to the mains.  "The technology comes from Germany," Sahin explains, noting my thick hair and fiddling with a heat dial to set the thermal scissor temperature accordingly to 130 degrees Fahrenheit (the range is from 10-150 degrees).  "Very few people do it and it's not an easy technique to learn. But I love the way it makes hair more healthy; it's a treatment as well as a cut because it stops the ends splitting," he continues. I am pleased to report there is no compromise on this new technique: it's magnificent and lasts. 

Do thermal scissors make a difference? 

Two months after stepping out of RYS, and I am thrilled as I don't have any split ends – long may this continue. The technique might require a greater outlay, but it is ideal for those who want to maximise time in between appointments, as the style lasts longer.  

Thermal Cut with Yilmaz, £250, at RYS Hair and Beauty, 176 Walton Street, London, SW3 2JL; 0207 100 0724; Ryshairandbeauty.com.
