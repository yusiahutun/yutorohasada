Bumble Launches Bizz, a Safe Career Networking App for Women

 Posted on May 15, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  368 words  |  Martina Birk

A safe space to network online.

It's a simple premise, but for the multitudes of women who report being routinely harassed by men on popular job-hunting sites like LinkedIn, it's one that holds a lot of promise—something that Bumble's latest app, Bumble Bizz, hopes to fulfill.

Courtesy of Bumble Bizz

In an effort to create a secure space for women to discuss their careers—and to curb the sexism and discrimination often found on professional networking sites—Bizz is putting emphasis on its “women first” experience, where, after swiping right on a prospective professional male connection, women have to be the first to send a direct message.

“We’ve stayed focused on creating a community with a foundation built upon positivity, respect, confidence and encouraging women to make the first move,” explained the founder and CEO of Bumble, Whitney Wolfe Herd.

Women were already networking professionally on Bumble BFF (an offshoot of Bumble's dating app that is focused on female friendship), says Wolfe Herd, so she simply evolved the product to match their expectations; adding to Bumble’s vision of becoming the ultimate social network, powered by the notion that one connection can lead to the opportunity of a lifetime.

The way Wolfe Herd sees it, Bumble modernized dating and Bizz will do the same for women’s professional lives—whether it's a woman who has just moved to a different city; someone leaving college to establish their career; a new parent on maternity leave; a female entrepreneur; or women who have been laid off, looking to retrain and start a new profession.

Courtesy of Bumble Bizz

"Love, friendship, networking—these are all critical connections and the foundation of a healthy, happy life," says Wolfe Herd. "We want to bring you closer to all of these connections, in an empowered way.”

Bumble Bizz will include a photo verification tool designed to help ensure people are who they say are. Beyond profile photos, a user’s Bumble Bizz profile will include a digital resume, in addition to a skills section showcasing the users’ talents and accolades. Users can also include examples of their work in additional profile cards.

Bumble is free and available from the App Store and is coming to Google Play on October 18.
