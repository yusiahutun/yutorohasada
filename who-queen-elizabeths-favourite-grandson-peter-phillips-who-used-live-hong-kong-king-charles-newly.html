Who is Peter Phillips to the late Queen Elizabeth?

Peter Phillips was eldest grandchild to the late Queen Elizabeth.

He is the son of the late queen’s sister Anne, Princess Royal, and her ex-husband, the equestrian Mark Phillips. Peter has a sister, Zara Tindall, and two half-siblings, Stephanie Phillips and Felicity Tonkin.

He counts the future king of England Prince William as a cousin, as well as Prince Harry, and has been pictured hanging out with both brothers and their respective spouses Kate Middleton and Meghan Markle over the years.

What does Peter Phillips do for work?

According to Hello!, he currently works as the head of partner acquisition for CSM Sport & Entertainment and is an ambassador for ISPS Handa, a Japanese non-profit that sponsors golf championships. In March, he attended the F1 Grand Prix in Bahrain.

Per British media, he previously held a job in hospitality management at Jaguar and worked for the Williams racing team as a sponsorship account manager.

Peter Phillips’ love life

Before his most recent girlfriend Lindsay Wallace, Phillips was married to Montreal-born Autumn Kelly, who he met at the 2003 Canadian Grand Prix, per British media. They tied the knot in 2008 at St George’s Chapel, Windsor, but ended things in 2020, and finalised their divorce a year later, according to Vanity Fair.

He used to live in Hong Kong

After tying the knot with Kelly, the then-newlyweds moved to Hong Kong, where Phillips was working for the Royal Bank of Scotland looking after sponsorship deals. He oversaw the bank’s Formula One motor racing deals in the region. The couple lived in Hong Kong for several years.

Phillips even appeared in a 2020 milk advert for Chinese company Bright Food, according to Metro.
