Robber posing as UPS delivery man is caught on camera breaking into Texas home

 Posted on May 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  615 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

This is the terrifying moment a man posing as a UPS driver broke into a house in Houston, Texas with three accomplices before the homeowner was beaten with a bat and Tasered four times.

Lawrence Berry fought the men just long enough for his wife and four-year-old twin daughters to get to a safe place in the house. 

The 'driver' rang the doorbell of the home on Willers Way, pretending he needed a signature for a package and, when Mr Berry opened the door, forced his way in.

In an interview with ABC 13, Mr Berry revealed that he had to receive 28 staples to the back of his scalp. He had stitches in his lip and a fractured skull as a result of the violent robbery. 

On November 22 around 8.40pm, a man dressed in a United Parcels Service (UPS) brown jacket and baseball cap - carrying a package - rang the doorbell and claimed he needed a signature for the package.

After Mr Berry answered his door, surveillance footage caught the horrifying moment that the 'UPS driver' forced his way into the house.

He was quickly followed by three other suspects wearing hoods or masks and with guns raised.

According to police, suspects assaulted a man inside the residence with a pistol and, while a shot was fired, no one was struck.
